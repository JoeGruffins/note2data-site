Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-06-02

Note2Data ("the app", "we", "us") is a handwriting recognition app that turns a photo of a handwritten page into editable text, entirely on your device. This policy explains what data the app processes, where it goes, and the choices you have.

The short version: your photos and recognized text never leave your phone. We do not run servers that store your data. The only data that leaves your device is anonymized crash reports (if you have not opted out via your operating system's settings) and purchase data handled by Apple or Google when you buy the Pro unlock. The app also downloads its recognition models once from our content host; that download contains no personal data.

1. Who we are

Note2Data is developed by Joseph Griffin (privacy@note2data.app), based in Okinawa, Japan. There is no company behind the app, no employees, and no marketing or data team.

2. Data that stays on your device

Everything below is processed locally on your phone and never transmitted off-device:

  • Photos of pages that you import, either via the camera or your photo library.
  • Recognized text produced by the on-device handwriting recognition engine.
  • Saved notes in your library, including any edits you make in the built-in text editor and any groups you create.
  • Read-aloud uses your device's built-in text-to-speech. The app passes the note's text to the operating system's speech engine and does not transmit it itself.
  • App settings and preferences, stored using the standard per-app storage provided by Android and iOS.

Exporting a note (as PDF, plain text, or a text file) hands the file to your operating system's share sheet, where you choose what to do with it. The app itself does not upload exported files anywhere.

When you uninstall the app, this data is removed by your operating system along with the app.

3. Data that leaves your device

The following limited data may leave your device:

Crash reports (Sentry)

If you experience a crash, the app may send a stack trace and basic device information (operating system version, app version, device model) to Sentry, a third-party error tracking service. This allows us to fix bugs.

Crash reports contain:

  • Stack traces (where in the code the crash happened)
  • App version and build mode
  • Device model and operating system version

Crash reports do NOT contain:

  • Your photos, recognized text, or saved notes
  • Personal identifiers such as your name or email
  • Screenshots
  • Location data

Sentry's privacy policy is at https://sentry.io/privacy/.

You can prevent crash reports from being sent by using your operating system's privacy controls (for example, restricting network access for the app) or by uninstalling the app.

Recognition models (content host)

The handwriting recognition models are large (around 743 MB) and run fully on your device. To avoid bundling them into the app download, they are fetched once on first run from our content delivery host and then cached on your device. This is a one-way download of static model files: the app sends no personal data as part of it. As with any download, the host can see your device's IP address and which files were requested. No account or identifier is involved.

Purchase data (Apple / Google)

When you purchase the Pro unlock, the transaction is handled entirely by Apple (on iOS) or Google (on Android). Note2Data does not see or store your payment details. We receive only a confirmation that you own the purchase, which is used to unlock the corresponding features in the app.

See Apple's privacy policy at https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/ and Google's at https://policies.google.com/privacy.

4. Permissions

The app asks for the following operating system permissions only when needed:

  • Camera: required to photograph a page for recognition.
  • Photo library: required when you import an existing photo of a page.

You can revoke any of these permissions in your operating system's settings at any time. The corresponding features will become unavailable, but the rest of the app continues to work.

5. Children

The app does not knowingly collect any personal information from children. It contains no advertising, no chat, no social features, no third-party trackers, and no user accounts. The optional crash reports described above contain no information that identifies a specific person.

If you are in the European Economic Area and your child is under 16, or in the United States and your child is under 13, your child may use the app under your supervision.

6. Your rights

Because Note2Data stores your photos, notes, and library entirely on your device, you control them directly through the app and through your operating system:

  • Access and export: open the note in the app, or export it as a PDF or text file from the library.
  • Deletion: delete notes in the app, or uninstall the app to remove all locally stored data.

If you live in the European Economic Area (GDPR), the United Kingdom (UK GDPR), California (CCPA / CPRA), or Japan (APPI), you have specific rights regarding the limited data that does leave your device (crash reports). To exercise these rights, contact us at the email below. Note that crash reports are not linked to your personal identity, so in practice we may not be able to identify which reports came from your device.

7. International data transfers

Crash reports sent to Sentry, and the one-time recognition-model download, may be processed or served on servers located outside your country of residence, including in the United States or the European Union. Sentry is the data processor for the crash-report data.

8. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the effective date at the top and, where reasonable, surface a notice in the app. Continued use of the app after a change indicates acceptance of the updated policy.

9. Contact
